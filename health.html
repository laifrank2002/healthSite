Risks of Using Drugs

Tobacco is a considered to be a gateway drug, meaning that people start out using something like tobacco, and soon make their way up to hardcore drugs, like cocaine. It's kind of like the transition between grade 8 and grade 9. It doesn't seem very long but there's a huge difference in your life afterwards.

Addiction

Tobacco is not something you would would like to try just for fun. There are serious consequences and health risks to using tobacco. A lot of these health risks are all sorts cancer, we all know these, but what are the less common consequences? A slowly increasing dependency on tobacco, or in other words, an addiction is the most life altering. Your behaviour around other people could change, people might not find you attractive anymore because you smell like smoke, and you could get fired from your job for smoking non-stop. There are limitless possibilities to the consequences of tobacco abuse. The point here is that 0 consequences (no drugs) is way better than limitless consequences.

How do we prevent that? The first step is avoidance: do not go near the substance. If you come into contact or possession of any quantity of tobacco, do not use it. Just one cigarette can make you addicted. If your friends are peer-pressuring you to try it, just walk away. People regret this for years upon. People in their 40s regret the habit they developed in their teenage years! Stay in well-lit areas, avoid smoking, smokers, and tobacco, and make friends who don't smoke. One mistake can change your life, and it's better you don't make it.
